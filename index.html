Bugatti Chiron

The Bugatti Chiron is a mid-engine two-seater sports car that epitomizes luxury and high performance. Introduced as the successor to the Veyron, the Chiron boasts an 8.0-liter quad-turbocharged W16 engine, delivering an impressive 1,479 horsepower (1,500 PS) and 1,600 Nm of torque. This powerhouse enables the Chiron to accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in just 2.5 seconds, with an electronically limited top speed of 420 km/h (261 mph). ​

In India, the Bugatti Chiron is expected to be priced starting at approximately ₹19.21 crore. However, due to import duties and customization options, the on-road price can be significantly higher. The Chiron is available in multiple variants, including the standard Chiron, Chiron Sport, and Chiron Super Sport, each offering unique features and performance enhancements. ​

Production of the Chiron is limited to 500 units globally, making it an exclusive and highly sought-after vehicle among automotive enthusiasts. Its combination of cutting-edge engineering, luxurious craftsmanship, and record-breaking performance solidifies the Bugatti Chiron's status as an iconic hypercar in the automotive world
